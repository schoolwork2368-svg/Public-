What are Whales

Whales are some of the many ocean dwellers in Earths oceans. There are different kinds of whales as well.

Click image below to see table

Whale Species Environment Size (Length)
Killer Whale Killer whales reside in all of the world oceans. The Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic oceans all house killer whales. Males: 20 - 26 feet
Females: 18 - 22 feet
Blue Whales Blue whales are found in all oceans except the Arctic ocean. They prefer deeper waters where they can find krill easier. Males: 65 - 80 feet
Females: 70 - 90 feet
Sperm Whales Sperm whales can be found in all oceans. They look like blue whales but have a square body. Males: 50 - 60 feet
Females: 30 - 40 feet
Humpback Whales Humpback whales belong to all the oceans and migrate between the Arctic and Pacific/Atlantic. Males: 40 - 48 feet
Females: 45 - 50 feet
